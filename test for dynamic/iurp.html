INSTITUTE OF URBAN AND REGIONAL PLANNING

The Centre for Urban Studies offered a Rachana Sansad Diploma in Urban Studies.

History

Mumbai, one of the largest urban agglomeration is constantly facing pressures of urbanisation. Rachana Sansad one of the pioneers in education in the field of Architecture and Design started a think tank along with Mumbai Study Group, which worked out the content to start the first school of Urban Planning in 2006, in Mumbai. The Centre for Urban Studies offered a Rachana Sansad Diploma in Urban Studies. However the students were keen to get a Masters degree in Urban and Regional Planning. So in 2008 the Institute of Urban and Regional Planning (IURP), was incepted. IURP, offers a two year Master's Programme in Urban and Regional Planning, affiliated to the YCMOU, Nashik and recognised and approved by Council of Architecture.



Our Philosophy

Recently the Urban Development Department has approved our course as an eligibility to apply for jobs of Planners in Government Departments of Maharashtra.

The journey from 2008 to 2014 has been a very enriching and exciting one. we started with a strength of 8 students in our first batch, soon the demand for the course saw a upward trend and we have had a good strength varying from 20 to 35 students per batch.

Academic Programme

The objectives of the Institute of Urban and Regional Planning are: To provide an in depth understanding of the complexities of built environment and its correlation to the socio-economic ethos.To provide practical guidance on navigating the legal and regulatory maze.The course is specially designed to train students in sustainable urban and regional practices by stressing on environmental issues and resource management.Lack of infrastructure and increase in densities in the developing countries like ours make it imperative to train planners in resource optimization.Technological advances need to be utilized keeping in mind the ecology and thus stressing in balanced development.

Eligibility

B.Arch or equivalent from a recognized University with min 50% aggregate in the final year.

M.Arch. (Urban And Regional Planning)

The M.Arch. (Urban And Regional Planning) program is a 2-year Masters Program affiliated to the Yashwantrao Chavan Maharashtra Open University (YCMOU), Nashik and recognized by the Council of Architecture (COA), New Delhi. It is conducted in 'Full time face to face counseling mode' over four semesters of 15 – 18 weeks each (with approximately 20 hours of contact sessions per week).

Admissions:

Rachana Sansad is a 50 year old institution of Mumbai and during its growth and expansion, it has gone beyond just being an educational institution in various ways. It has not only been a pivot in creative education but has also provided a platform for NGOs, citizens, public sector and community to interact on policy and city developmental matters.  The unique role of Rachana Sansad is that its approach has consistently been inclusive in nature. As the new development plan is in the process of preparation for MMR and with the introduction of schemes such as JNNURM , public agencies are depending largely on private and civil society groups to play a fellow researcher and services consultant role in project designing, implementation and monitoring. Rachana Sansad with its milieu and pedigree has a huge institutional and human resource advantage which can contribute to this process as well as gain knowledge from it. 

We believe that transformation in a city can take place only with creation of good public spaces that build local value and meet community needs. We want to become a recognized center for information, resources and best-practices, for converting  "Urban spaces to Urban Places". Our vision is to act as the central hub of this  "Place making movement".  We believe that to create a meaningful urban place the most important starting point is to understand the critical issues of the surrounding fabric and arrive at what is meaningful for the community. Any vision and program developed on the basis of community participation ultimately helps to create a sense of ownership in the development process and product. We believe that to create a meaningful urban place the most important starting point is to understand the critical issues of the surrounding fabric and what is meaningful for the community.  The vision and the program developed on the basis of community participation will help to create a sense of ownership in the project. We want to create places not designs. The goal is to create places that have a strong sense of community, places which are lively, secure and have a distinct identity. A place which is democratic that simultaneously addresses multiple interests and concerns. 

Rachana Sansad facilitates a team of professionals, who work under the Institute of Urban and Regional Planning, concentrating on urban research issues and design projects within and outside the city of Mumbai. This group is a team of highly qualified professionals with expertise in diverse fields who are trained in architecture, urban planning, urban design, and environmental planning.
